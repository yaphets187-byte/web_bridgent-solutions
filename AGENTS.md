# AGENTS.md

## Mission

This repository powers the official website of Bridgent Solutions.

The purpose of this site is not to impress people with flashy web design.
The purpose is to make visitors quickly understand:
- who we are
- what we build
- who we help
- why they should trust us
- what they should do next

Optimize in this order:
1. clarity
2. trust
3. correctness
4. maintainability
5. conversion
6. performance
7. visual polish

Do not optimize for cleverness.
Do not add complexity to a simple business website.

---

## About Bridgent Solutions

Bridgent Solutions builds lightweight digital solutions for operational teams and SMEs.

The company focus is:
- internal tools
- workflow digitalization
- operational dashboards
- automation
- process simplification
- practical systems that solve real pain points

Brand positioning:
Bridgent Solutions sits between manual workflows and overly heavy enterprise software.
The company should feel practical, sharp, operational, modern, and trustworthy.

This is not a hype-AI brand.
This is not a vague "innovation consultancy".
This is a real-world problem-solving business.

## Brand Principles

The website must communicate these brand qualities consistently:
- practical
- clear
- reliable
- modern
- operationally grounded
- low-bullshit
- solution-oriented

Avoid brand tone that feels:
- bloated
- vague
- corporate for the sake of sounding big
- overhyped
- too startup-gimmicky
- too technical for normal business visitors

The tone should feel like:
- a smart operator
- a practical builder
- a business-minded technical partner

## Core Messaging Rules

Every important page should help visitors answer these questions fast:

1. What does Bridgent Solutions do?
2. Who is it for?
3. What pain does it solve?
4. Why not continue doing it manually?
5. Why not use an oversized enterprise system?
6. Why should I trust Bridgent Solutions?
7. What should I do next?

Copy should be:
- direct
- concrete
- easy to scan
- low on jargon
- outcome-focused

Do not write empty phrases like:
- "empowering transformation"
- "unlocking innovation"
- "next-generation synergy"
- "revolutionizing the future"

If the sentence sounds fancy but says nothing, delete it.

## Site Priorities

The website exists to:
- build trust
- explain services clearly
- position the company intelligently
- convert interest into inquiry
- show practical capability

Prioritize:
- clear headlines
- simple structure
- visible CTA
- readable service explanations
- mobile clarity
- trust-building sections
- fast loading
- strong first impression without fluff

Do not prioritize:
- trendy animation
- fancy effects
- gimmicky layouts
- clever but confusing wording
- style at the cost of comprehension

---

## UX Rules

Prefer:
- obvious navigation
- strong section hierarchy
- clean spacing
- readable text width
- clear CTA placement
- predictable behavior
- mobile-first clarity
- short paths to inquiry

Avoid:
- hidden important information
- overloaded homepages
- carousels unless strongly justified
- interaction tricks that weaken trust
- decorative motion that slows reading
- text embedded in images
- obscure icons without labels

If something looks cooler but reduces clarity, reject it.

---

## Design Rules

The visual design should support trust and seriousness.

The site should feel:
- clean
- calm
- professional
- modern
- operational
- premium in discipline, not in excess

Do not make the site feel:
- childish
- noisy
- over-decorated
- overly dark and aggressive unless intentionally justified
- generic template-like if a clearer branded structure is possible

Visual consistency matters more than visual novelty.

---

## CTA Rules

Every core page should have an obvious next step.

Preferred CTA directions include:
- Book a consultation
- Tell us your workflow problem
- Request a discussion
- Explore solutions
- Contact us

CTA language should be:
- clear
- direct
- low-friction
- business-like

Avoid weak CTA wording that feels passive or vague.

---

## Content Rules

When writing or revising copy:
- speak in outcomes, not buzzwords
- describe real problems and practical solutions
- keep paragraphs scannable
- make sections earn their space
- remove filler aggressively

Preferred content angles:
- operational clarity
- workflow simplification
- right-sized digitalization
- practical automation
- internal tool design
- reducing manual friction
- bridging the gap between chaos and overkill software

Do not let the copy drift into "AI because AI" language.

---

## SEO Rules

Protect fundamentals:
- semantic heading structure
- strong page titles
- useful meta descriptions
- crawlable content
- descriptive link text
- alt text where appropriate
- mobile usability
- performance

Do not stuff keywords unnaturally.
Do not sacrifice readability for SEO hacks.

---

## Technical Rules

Prefer:
- simple component structure
- semantic HTML
- accessible forms and buttons
- reusable sections where useful
- low dependency count
- maintainable styling
- performant assets

Avoid:
- over-engineering
- unnecessary client-side state
- heavy animation libraries
- bloated packages for small visual gains
- clever architecture with weak payoff

A company site should be easy to maintain by a small business.

---

## Form / Inquiry Flow Rules

If the site includes contact or inquiry forms:

Always:
- keep fields minimal
- make submission status obvious
- handle errors visibly
- prevent duplicate submissions
- keep alternative contact options visible
- reduce friction

Never:
- ask for too much too early
- leave users unsure whether the form worked
- trap users in a dead-end flow

---

## High-Risk Areas

Treat the following as sensitive:
- homepage hero
- company positioning copy
- service descriptions
- CTA wording
- navigation
- contact flow
- brand assets
- SEO metadata
- deployment config
- analytics tracking

Any change in these areas should be deliberate and small.

---

## Repo Discipline

Touch only what is necessary.

Do not:
- redesign unrelated sections during a small task
- rewrite site-wide wording casually
- create abstraction layers for simple pages
- introduce dependencies without clear business value
- mix design cleanup, SEO changes, and feature work in one patch unless necessary

Keep diffs reviewable and easy to reason about.

---

## Validation Standard

Before finalizing, validate using the strongest available method:

1. check the affected page structure
2. check headline clarity
3. check CTA visibility
4. check mobile readability
5. check navigation flow
6. check responsive layout
7. check SEO basics if affected
8. check form flow if affected
9. check visual consistency with the rest of the site

Report:
- what changed
- why it improves the site
- what was validated
- what remains unverified

Never claim business impact like "higher conversion" unless there is evidence.
Use honest wording such as:
- "likely improves clarity"
- "should reduce friction"
- "makes the CTA more obvious"

---

## Communication Style

For non-trivial tasks, respond in this structure:

1. Understanding
2. Plan
3. Changes made
4. Validation
5. Remaining risks or follow-ups

Be concise.
Be honest.
Do not oversell weak improvements.

---

## Non-Negotiables

Never casually:
- overcomplicate a simple business website
- weaken clarity for style
- bury the CTA
- write vague corporate fluff
- add heavy dependencies for minor effects
- break mobile layout
- change brand tone inconsistently
- claim strong impact without evidence

When in doubt:
- choose clarity over novelty
- choose trust over flash
- choose simple structure over clever design
- choose maintainability over overdesign
- choose truth over confidence
